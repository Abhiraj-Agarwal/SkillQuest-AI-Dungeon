# Gemini prompt — background music for SkillQuest: The AI Dungeon

Copy everything below into Gemini (or whichever audio-generation tool you're using it through).

---

I'm building a pixel-art dungeon-crawler learning game called **SkillQuest: The AI Dungeon** — a data-structures-and-algorithms RPG where you fight topic-themed monsters by answering real questions correctly. The whole visual identity is a damp, torch-lit cave: near-black stone, rusted iron chains, ember-orange torchlight, teal arcane glow, occasional bats swooping through. I need background music that matches that world.

## Overall mood

Spooky, atmospheric dungeon-crawler ambience — think a cross between a roguelike's exploration theme and a haunted-cave soundscape. **Tense but not exhausting**: this plays on a loop behind someone reading and answering questions, so it needs to support concentration, not fight for attention. No jump-scares, no aggressive stingers, no vocals/lyrics.

## What I need — 3 separate tracks

1. **Dungeon exploration (main loop).** The default ambient track for browsing the topic map and reading questions. Slow, sparse, minor-key. Low drones and distant dripping-water/cave-echo textures underneath, occasional far-off chain-rattle or bat-screech as a rare accent (not rhythmic, just atmosphere). A faint, slow melodic motif on something like a detuned music box, hollow flute, or bowed instrument, spaced far apart so it doesn't loop-fatigue. Tempo: roughly 60-75 BPM, no strong beat. **Must loop seamlessly** — the ending should flow back into the beginning with no audible seam. Target length: 90-180 seconds before it loops.

2. **Combat (topic room fight).** A bit more tension than the exploration loop but still restrained — this plays while answering a question against a room's villain. Add a low, steady pulse (like a slow heartbeat or a distant war-drum) under the same cave-drone palette, subtle rising string or synth swell, more presence in the low end. Should feel like "something is watching and waiting for your answer," not full battle-music energy. Same seamless-loop requirement, similar length.

3. **Boss fight (final encounter).** The most intense of the three, for the "Big-O Devourer" final boss — a colossal multi-headed dragon/demon. Bigger low-end (deep brass or distorted low synth), a heavier and more insistent percussive pulse, occasional dissonant choir-like swell or metallic shriek for punctuation, still no vocals. This should clearly feel like the highest-stakes moment in the game while remaining loopable and not overwhelming underneath on-screen text.

## Technical requirements

- Instrumental only, no lyrics or vocal melody.
- Each track must **loop cleanly** (no fade-in/fade-out, no dead air at the start/end that would create a stutter when it repeats).
- Deliver as separate audio files (WAV or high-quality MP3), one per track above.
- Roughly consistent loudness across all three so switching between them (exploration -> combat -> boss) doesn't jar the ear with a volume jump.

## What to send back

Three audio files, one per section above, clearly labeled (exploration / combat / boss). Send them back here and I'll handle looping them in the app myself.
